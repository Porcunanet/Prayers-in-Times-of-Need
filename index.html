Introduction

We pray in order to communicate with God, to learn His will for us, and to offer ourselves to Him. God always answers prayer. He may not, however, answer the way we want Him to. While it is true that Jesus said if we ask anything in His name we will receive it, He also said, "Now is the confidence that we have in Him, that if we ask anything according to His will, He hears us." How do we learn to pray "according to His will"? At the beginning stages of prayer, using written prayers is like having training wheels on a bicycle. Using prayers that have been hallowed by time and use help us become rooted in the mind of the Church and the mind of Christ.

These prayers are some of the most basic prayers of Orthodox Christians throughout the centuries. Take time over the words of the prayers. Say them carefully and reverently. Think about what the words mean. Allow them to sink into your soul. You will find that these prayers will help you communicate with God, and praying will give you great help in time of need.

Intercessions for the Living and the Departed

Lord Jesus Christ, remember Your Holy, Catholic, and Apostolic Church, which You redeemed with Your precious blood. Keep it strong and growing; Keep it in peace.

Lord, have mercy and save our rulers and all who protect us, that we may have a tranquil life, free to pursue right belief, virtue and godly living.

Lord, have mercy and save the holy patriarchs, bishops, and clergy of Your church. Through their prayers have mercy on me, a sinner, and save me.

Lord, have mercy and save my family (parents, brothers, sisters, spouse, children and others) and my friends (names). Give them blessings now and salvation in the life to come.

Lord, have mercy and save:

  • The very old and the very young, including the unborn (names)
  • The needy, the orphans, and the widows (names)
  • The sick and afflicted (names)
  • Those in sorrow and distress (names)
  • The imprisoned, and all Orthodox Christians who suffer for Your sake (names)
  • Those in military service (names)
  • Travelers (names)
  • (And others who come to mind) Remember them, and come to them with strength and comfort, and quickly deliver them.

Lord, remember all those who have fallen asleep in the hope of resurrection and eternal life. Forgive all their sins committed in knowledge or ignorance. Let them enjoy Your eternal blessings in the Light of Your presence; Grant them peace, refreshment, fellowship with Your saints, and life that never ends.

Have mercy on us, for You are full of grace and love mankind. Amen.

The Penitential Troparia

Have mercy on us, O Lord, have mercy on us; for we sinners, who are without means of defense offer You our Master this supplication, have mercy on us.

Glory to the Father and to the Son and to the Holy Spirit

Lord, have mercy on us, for in You we trust. Do not be exceedingly angry with us, nor remember our iniquities. But look upon us now in Your compassion and deliver us from our enemies. For You are our God, and we are Your people; we are all the works of Your hands, and we call upon Your Name.

Now and ever and unto ages of ages. Amen

Open the gate of your compassion to us, O blessed Mother of God, and hoping in you may we not fail. Through you may we be delivered from adversity, for you are the salvation of the Christian race.

Brief Prayers for Morning and Evening

In the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit. Amen

Having invoked the Holy Trinity, be still so that your thoughts may be freed from worldly cares. Make the sign of the cross and bow three times, saying each time the prayer of the publican: "O God, be merciful to me, a sinner."

A Morning Prayer

by Saint Philaret of Moscow

O Lord, grant me to greet the coming day in peace, help me in all things to rely upon Your holy will. In every hour of the day reveal Your will to me. Bless my dealings with all who surround me. Teach me to treat all that comes to me throughout the day with peace of soul and with firm conviction that Your will governs all. In all my deeds and words, guide my thoughts and feelings. In unforeseen events, let me not forget that all are sent by You. Teach me to act firmly and wisely, without embittering and embarrassing others. Give me strength to bear the fatigue of the coming day with all that it shall bring. Direct my will, teach me to pray. And You, Yourself, pray in me. Amen.

An Evening Prayer

O eternal God, King of all creation, who have granted me to attain to this hour: forgive me the sins which I have committed this day in thought, word, and deed; and cleanse, O Lord, my humble soul from every stain of flesh and spirit. Grant me, O Lord, to pass through the sleep of this night in peace, that when I rise from my bed I may please Your holy Name all the days of my life, and conquer the enemies, both corporeal and incorporeal, that contend against me. Deliver me, O Lord, from the vain thoughts that stain me, and from evil desires. For Yours is the kingdom and the power and the glory of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, now and ever and unto ages of ages. Amen.

Prayers for Specific Needs

A Prayer in Time of Illness

O Lord our God, who by a word alone did heal all diseases, who did cure the kinswoman of Peter, You who chastise with pity and heal according to Your goodness; who are able to put aside every sickness and infirmity, do You Yourself, the same Lord, grant aid to Your servant, (name) and cure him/her of every sickness of which he/she is grieved; and send down upon him/her Your great mercy. And if it be Your will, give to him/her health and a complete recovery; for You are the Physician of our souls and bodies, and to You do we send up glory to the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, both now and forever, and to the ages of ages. Amen.

A Prayer before Beginning a Task

Almighty God, our Help and Refuge, the Fountain of wisdom and Tower of strength, who know that I can do nothing without Your guidance and help; assist me, I pray, and direct me to divine wisdom and power, that I may accomplish this task, and whatever I may undertake to do, faithfully and diligently, according to Your will, so that it may be profitable to myself and others, and to the glory of Your Holy Name. For Yours is the kingdom, and the power, and the glory, of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit: now and ever, and unto ages of ages. Amen.
